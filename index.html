KNOWING OUR UNIVERSE

The universe, an infinite and mysterious space, full of wonders that are still waiting to be discovered. From the brightest stars to the most distant galaxies, our cosmos is home to an unimaginable variety of celestial objects that invite exploration and wonder.

The Universe

The universe is the totality of space and time, of all forms of matter, energy, momentum, the physical laws that govern them, and the physical constants. It contains galaxies, stars, planets, moons, asteroids, comets, dust and gas, and possibly other undiscovered objects.

The Planets

Planets are fascinating objects that have been studied for centuries. As we learn more about them, we continue to discover new secrets about their formation, evolution and composition.

Mercury

The planet closest to the Sun, small and rocky.

Venus

A hot, dense planet with a thick atmosphere of toxic gases.

Earth

The only planet known to harbor life.

Mars

A rocky planet with a thin atmosphere and extinct volcanoes.

Jupiter

The largest planet in the Solar System, a gas giant with a strong magnetic field.

Saturn

A gas giant with prominent rings made of ice and dust.

Uranus

An icy giant with an atmosphere rich in hydrogen and helium.

Neptune

An icy giant with a blue atmosphere and strong winds.
